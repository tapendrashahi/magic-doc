Ellipse

An ellipse is the locus of a point P(x, y) in a plane such that the sum of its distances from two fixed points is always constant.

A conic section having eccentricity less than one (e < 1) is called an ellipse.

The ratio of the distance of any point on the ellipse from a fixed point (focus) to its distance from a fixed line (directrix) is constant and less than 1.

SP / PM = e, where e < 1.

General Equation

The general second-degree equation

ax² + 2hxy + by² + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0

represents an ellipse if:

  • Δ ≠ 0
  • h² < ab

When eccentricity becomes zero, the ellipse reduces to a circle.

Standard Equation of Ellipse (a > b)

x²/a² + y²/b² = 1

  • Length of major axis = 2a
  • Length of minor axis = 2b
  • Major axis along x-axis
  • Minor axis along y-axis
  • Centre = (0, 0)
  • Foci = (±ae, 0)
  • Vertices = (±a, 0)
  • Directrices: x = ±a/e
  • Latus rectum length = 2b² / a
  • Latus rectum equations: x = ±ae

Eccentricity: e = c / a, where b² = a² − c²

Example

Find the length of the major and minor axis of the ellipse:

x²/25 + y²/9 = 1

a² = 25 ⇒ a = 5
b² = 9 ⇒ b = 3

Major axis = 2a = 10
Minor axis = 2b = 6

Vertical Ellipse (b > a)

x²/a² + y²/b² = 1

  • Major axis along y-axis
  • Minor axis along x-axis
  • Centre = (0, 0)
  • Foci = (0, ±be)
  • Vertices = (0, ±b)
  • Directrices: y = ±b/e
  • Latus rectum length = 2a² / b
  • Latus rectum equations: y = ±ae

Eccentricity: e = √(1 − a²/b²)

Ellipse with Centre (h, k)

(x − h)²/a² + (y − k)²/b² = 1

  • Centre = (h, k)
  • Major axis length = 2a
  • Minor axis length = 2b
  • Foci = (h ± ae, k)
  • Vertices = (h ± a, k)
  • Latus rectum length = 2b² / a

Parametric Form of Ellipse

x = a cosθ
y = b sinθ

Squaring and adding:

x²/a² + y²/b² = 1

Hence, the curve represents an ellipse.

Tangent to the Ellipse

Tangent at point (x₁, y₁):

x·x₁/a² + y·y₁/b² = 1

Tangent at parametric point (a cosθ, b sinθ):

(x/a)cosθ + (y/b)sinθ = 1

Tangent in Slope Form

The equation of the tangent to the ellipse is:

y = mx ± √(a²m² + b²)

Important Results

  • Distance between foci = 2ae
  • Distance between directrices = 2a/e
  • Sum of focal distances = 2a
  • If a = b, ellipse becomes a circle
  • Auxiliary circle: x² + y² = a²

Position of a Point

If substituting a point (x, y) in the ellipse equation gives:

  • < 1 → point lies inside
  • = 1 → point lies on the ellipse
  • > 1 → point lies outside
