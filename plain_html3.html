Trigonometric equation:

An equation involving one or more trigonometric functions of a variable is known as trigonometric equation.

Principal value:

The value of the trigonometric function between 0 to $2 \pi$ is known as principal value.

General values:

The solution generalized by means of periodicity is known as general values.

Example:-

If $\tan (\theta+\alpha) \cdot \tan (\theta-\alpha)=1$ for all $\alpha \in \mathbf{R}$, then $\theta$ is equal to $(n \in I)$ a) $2 n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{4}$ b) $n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{4}$ c) $n \pi \pm(-1) \frac{\pi}{4}$ d) $n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{6}$

Solution:-

$$ \begin{aligned} & \frac{\sin (\theta+\alpha) \cdot \sin (\theta-\alpha)}{\cos (\theta+\alpha) \cos (\theta-\alpha)}=1 \\ & \sin ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \alpha=\cos ^{2} \theta-\sin ^{2} \alpha \\ & \tan ^{2} \theta=1 \\ & \theta=n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{4} \end{aligned} $$

Example:-

The general solutions of $2 \sin ^{2} x+\sin ^{2} 2 x=2$ is a) $(2 n+1) \pi / 4$ b) $2 n \pi \pm \pi / 4$ c) $n \pi+\pi / 3$ d) $n \pi / 4$

Solution:-

$$ \begin{aligned} & 2 \sin ^{2} x+\sin ^{2} 2 x=2 \\ & (1-\cos 2 x)+\left(1-\cos ^{2} 2 x\right)=2 \\ & -\cos 2 x(1+\cos 2 x)=0 \\ & \therefore \cos 2 x=0 \text { or } \\ & \cos 2 x=-1 \end{aligned} $$ i.e. $2 x=(2 n+1) \pi / 2$ or $2 \mathrm{x}=(2 \mathrm{n}+1) \pi$ i.e. $x=(2 n+1) \pi / 4$ $$ x=(2 n+1) \pi / 2 $$

Example:-

The set of values of x for which $$ \frac{\tan 3 x-\tan 2 x}{1+\tan 3 x \tan 2 x}=1 \text { is } $$ a) $\phi$ b) $\left\{\frac{\pi}{4}\right\}$ c) $\left\{n \pi+\frac{\pi}{4}, n=1,2,3 \cdots, \cdots\right.$ d) $\left\{2 n \pi+\frac{\pi}{4}: n=1,2,3 \cdots-\cdots\right\}$

Solution:-

$$ \begin{aligned} & \frac{\tan 3 x-\tan 2 x}{1+\tan 3 x \tan 2 x}=1 \\ & \tan (3 x-2 x)=1 \\ & \tan x=1 \end{aligned} $$

But for $\tan \mathrm{x}=1, \tan 2 \mathrm{x}=\frac{2 \tan x}{1-\tan ^{2} x}$ does not exist Hence, it has no solution.

Example:-

The general solutions of $\sin 2 x-4 \sin x-\cos x+2=0$ is a) $n \pi+(-1)^{n} \frac{\pi}{6}$ b) $2 n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{4}$ c) $n \pi+\frac{\pi}{4}$ d) $\cos ^{-1} 2$

Solution:-

$$ \begin{aligned} & 2 \sin x \cos x-4 \sin x-\cos x+2=0 \\ & \cos x(2 \sin x-1)-2(2 \sin x-1)=0 \\ & (2 \sin x-1)(\cos x-2)=0 \end{aligned} $$
Either, $2 \sin x=1$ $$ \begin{aligned} & \sin x=\frac{1}{2}=\sin \frac{\pi}{6} \\ & x=n \pi+(-1)^{\prime \prime} \frac{\pi}{6} \end{aligned} $$

Note: $\cos ^{-1} 2$ is not defined

Example:-

If $\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\theta\right)+\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\theta\right)=4$ then the general values of $\theta=$ a) $n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{3}$ b) $n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{6}$ c) $n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{4}$ d) $2 n \pi+\frac{\pi}{4}$

Solution:-

$$ \begin{aligned} & \tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}+\theta\right)+\tan \left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\theta\right)=4 \\ & \frac{\tan \frac{\pi}{4}+\tan \theta}{1-\tan \frac{\pi}{4} \cdot \tan \theta}+\frac{\tan \frac{\pi}{4}-\tan \theta}{1+\tan \frac{\pi}{4} \cdot \tan \theta}=4 \\ & \frac{1+\tan \theta}{1-\tan \theta}+\frac{1-\tan \theta}{1+\tan \theta}=4 \\ & \tan ^{2} \theta=1 / 3 \\ & \theta=n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{6} \end{aligned} $$

Example:-

The equation $\sin x+\cos x=2$ has: a) unique solution b) no solution c) finite solution d) infinite solution

Solution:-

$$ \sin x+\cos x=2 $$
Dividing by $\sqrt{1^{2}+1^{2}}=\sqrt{2}$ we get, $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \sin x+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \cos x=\frac{2}{\sqrt{2}}$ $$ \begin{aligned} & \cos \frac{\pi}{4} \sin x+\sin \frac{\pi}{4} \cos x=\sqrt{2} \\ & \sin \left(x+\frac{\pi}{4}\right)=\sqrt{2}>1 . \text { No solution } \end{aligned} $$

Example:-

The general solution of $\cos \theta=\cos 105^{\circ}+\sin 105^{\circ}$ is a) $2 n \pi \pm \pi / 4$ b) $2 n \pi \pm \pi / 3$ c) $n \pi+\pi / 3$ d) none

Solution:

$$ \begin{aligned} \cos \theta & =\sin 105^{\circ}+\cos \left(90^{\circ}+15^{\circ}\right) \\ \cos \theta & =\sin 105^{\circ}-\sin 15^{\circ} \\ & =2 \cos \left(\frac{105^{\circ}+15^{\circ}}{2}\right) \cdot \sin \left(\frac{105^{\circ}-15^{\circ}}{2}\right) \\ & =2 \cos 60^{\circ} \cdot \sin 45^{\circ} \\ & =2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ & =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ & =\cos \frac{\pi}{4} \\ \therefore \theta & =2 n \pi \pm \frac{\pi}{4} \end{aligned} $$

Example:-

The general solution of $$ 2 \sin ^{2} x+\sqrt{3} \cos x+1=0 $$ a) $2 n \pi+\pi / 6$ b) $2 n \pi+5 \pi / 6$ c) $n \pi+\pi / 6$ d) $n \pi+5 \pi / 6$

Solution:-

$$ \begin{aligned} & 2 \sin ^{2} x+\sqrt{3} \cos x+1=0 \\ & 2-2 \cos ^{2} x+\sqrt{3} \cos x+1=0 \\ & -2 \cos ^{2} x+\sqrt{3} \cos x+3=0 \\ & 2 \cos ^{2} x-\sqrt{3} \cos x-3=0 \\ & 2 \cos ^{2} x-2 \sqrt{3} \cos x+\sqrt{3} \cos x-3=0 \\ & 2 \cos x(\cos x-\sqrt{3})+\sqrt{3}(\cos x-\sqrt{3})=0 \\ & (2 \cos x+\sqrt{3})(\cos x-\sqrt{3})=0 \end{aligned} $$
Now, $$ \begin{aligned} & 2 \cos x+\sqrt{3}=0 \text { gives } \\ & \cos x=-\sqrt{3} / 2=\cos 5 \pi / 6 \\ & x=2 n \pi \pm 5 \pi / 6 \end{aligned} $$
