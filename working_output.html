1. A binomial is an algebraic expression of two terms which are connected by the operations ' + ' or ' - '. e.g. (x+2)

2. Binomial Theorem:

The binomial theorem for natural numbers states that -

For any positive integer n, the expansion of

(a+x)n = nC0anx0 + nC1an-1x + nC2an-2x2 + …

Note:

  1. Total number of terms after binomial expansion of (a+x)n is (n+1)
  2. Sum of the exponents in each term is n
  3. Powers of a are in descending order and the powers of x are in ascending order

General Term:

(i) (r+1)th term in the expansion of (a+x)n is

tr+1 = nCran-rxr

(ii) General term in the expansion of (a−x)n is

tr+1 = (−1)rC(n,r)an-rxr

Middle Term:

(i) If n is even:

tn/2+1 = C(n, n/2)an/2xn/2

(ii) If n is odd:

There are two middle terms: (n+1)/2th and ((n+1)/2 + 1)th

Some Special Cases:

  • (1−x)−1 = 1 + x + x2 + x3 + …
  • (1+x)−1 = 1 − x + x2 − x3 + …
  • (1−x)−2 = 1 + 2x + 3x2 + …
  • (1+x)−2 = 1 − 2x + 3x2 − …

Binomial Coefficients:

The coefficients C(n,0), C(n,1), … C(n,n) are called binomial coefficients.

  • C0 = 1
  • C1 = n
  • C2 = n(n−1)/2
  • Cn = 1

Important Results:

  • Sum of binomial coefficients = 2n
  • Sum of even coefficients = sum of odd coefficients = 2n−1
  • Total terms in (x+y+z)n = (n+1)(n+2)/2
